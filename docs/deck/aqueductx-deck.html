02The problem

Judgment doesn't scale down.

Between the third of the world's food that smallholders grow and the markets that consume it stands a chain of intermediaries, because trade runs on judgment. A smallholder cannot carry this operational cost.

$2.5T
The global trade-finance gap—demand for credit that goes unmet worldwide.
ADB Trade Finance Gaps Survey, 2023 & Jan 2026
$250
The minimum inspection fee per shipment—a smallholder lot cannot carry it.
Bureau Veritas Verigates PVoC fee schedule
$323B
Smallholder finance demanded a year—against ~$95B supplied.
ISF Advisors Global Report, 2025

Lenders do not lend against lots they cannot verify—legibility is the bottleneck before capital.

04What it is

A swarm decision-support system for agricultural trade finance.

A swarm of single-purpose agents reads real platform surfaces, verifies each lot, prices it against a real floor, and hands a person the financing or sale decision to settle.

Compliance is one axis, not the product. Standards surface as cited conditions on routes and solver bids; three resolvers are registered—TIC certifiers, GLOW-GCA, GIIN—and EUDR is the live instance, not the category.

07Data model

The data model is REA—the Claim is the point.

AqueductX's types were already an unlabeled Resource–Event–Agent model (McCarthy 1982; Valueflows). Labeling it turns "map the value chain" from a slogan into schema decisions, and surfaced the one object that was missing: the Claim, financing's deferred half.

Sell and finance are one schema, not two features. Resources sit on both sides—the ontology models the smallholder economy whole: what the farm sells, and what it consumes to produce.
