02The problem

Judgment doesn't scale down.

Between the third of the world's food that smallholders grow and the markets that consume it stands a chain of intermediaries — and the chain exists because trade runs on judgment. Somebody has to grade the goods, vouch for their origin, know the buyers, price the lot, and carry the risk of moving it. A shipload can carry the cost of that judgment; a smallholder's lot cannot. So the producers with the least leverage trade the least legible goods and pay the most for the trust their trade requires.

03Why now

Two clocks just aligned: a legal deadline, and a collapse in the cost of judgment.

The legal clock

The EU Deforestation Regulation makes plot-level origin proof a legal requirement for coffee and cocoa entering the EU — large operators from 30 December 2026, micro and small enterprises from 30 June 2027. But buyers and importers are on the 2026 clock and already demand origin proof from their suppliers, so for the smallholder market access, not the statute, is the effective deadline. It converts lot legibility into priced market access, and the industry is rebuilding its origin infrastructure this year. Rebuilds like this happen once a generation, and the standards chosen during them persist — the open version has not been built by anyone.

The cost clock

Aggregating, verifying, pricing and matching are exactly the tasks AI now performs cheaply at any scale — so the logistics layer of an entire industry is about to become software. But the unlock is acting on price, not knowing it:

+13–14%
Peru — SMS price info raised farmer prices, because a reachable alternative buyer existed.
Nakasone, RCT, 2014
0%
India — the identical SMS treatment moved nothing. Information with nowhere to take it.

04What it is

A swarm decision-support system for agricultural trade finance.

Not another marketplace. Aggregate, verify, price, match are classic decision-support functions — performed here by a swarm of small, single-purpose agents instead of one intermediary — feeding a capital-allocation decision that a person or an institution still owns. Settlement is the action taken. A marketplace intermediates; a DSS informs a decision someone else makes.

  • Agents aggregate — scouts read the platforms where smallholder lots already live.
  • Agents verify — diligence agents re-check origin and grade, rendering gaps honestly.
  • Agents price — oracles hold a real floor per commodity and expose the spread.
  • Intents match — solvers compete to fill with itemized landed-cost bids.
  • Settlement acts — a filled intent settles on-chain, crediting the coop node.

06Data model

The data model is REA — and the Claim is the whole point.

AqueductX's types were already an unlabeled Resource–Event–Agent model (McCarthy 1982; Valueflows). Labeling it turns "map the value chain" from a slogan into schema decisions — and surfaced the one object that was missing: the Claim, financing's deferred half.

A sell-this-lot intent is near-spot: lot for capital, concurrent. A finance-this-planting intent is not — capital moves now, repayment later. Both are the same species of exchange; they differ only in which side is deferred. Sell and finance are one schema, not two features.

10Differentiator · privacy

Compliance without exposure — the open version must not be a surveillance layer.

A swarm that coordinates on public data is just a faster marketplace: visible bids get shaded against, visible reserves feed buyer power, visible farm polygons get targeted. Institutional knowledge should be "sufficient to enable a right, a benefit, or a protective measure" — and no more, never enough to make a producer's whole life more linkable. EUDR geolocation reaches competent authorities only, via TRACES/DDS — never the public or the buyer chain.

Real today — privacy by subtraction
  • EUDR status is never a polygon. The map derives ready / partial / gap without ever holding plot geometry — EUDR does not require polygons to be public.
  • Initials by design. Lots carry redacted names; the person is not a demo prop.
  • Zero new cryptography — fully real, shipped as policy, not a claim.

12Precedents

Built before, in pieces — and one negative precedent we learn from.

LVGA / Lugano
City-issued token, FINMA-supervised, deployed: endogenous circulation (the coop-ring economics) + conditional transfers riding in the instrument — 650 cultural vouchers, CHF 130k, ERC-2980. Proof the mechanisms we render as sim are already live public infrastructure.
Kiva
The single-sentence ask-card convention — "€1,120 helps this community plant 1,200 seedlings." Honestly ours: an AqueductX-designed convention over real computed numbers, since no platform publishes a per-farmer ask card.
EthicHub
The real anchor. The only live smallholder-native collateral model, with real repaid credit lines. A legible lot lets the crop itself carry the loan — the upgrade AqueductX proposes over the venues it aggregates.
Marco Polo — the negative precedent
Trade-finance networks that led with a graph metaphor went insolvent; the survivors (C2FO, Taulia) present named rate tiers and decomposed risk. We present decisions, not network theater. The map is a reading instrument, not the claim to intermediation.
