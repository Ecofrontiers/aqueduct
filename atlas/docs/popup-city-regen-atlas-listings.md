# Pop-up City Ecosystem: Potential Regen Atlas Org Listings

> Research conducted 2026-06-01. Sources: xyz.city directory (35+ catalogued network states/pop-up cities), arxiv:2511.15680 (Social Layer paper), Regen Coordination Hub, ReFi DAO ecosystem, Terrenity research, and direct web research.

## Context

The pop-up city movement (tracked by [xyz.city](https://xyz.city)) has catalogued 100+ network states and pop-up villages globally. Most focus on crypto/AI/longevity, but a growing subset is explicitly regenerative, ecological, or governance-oriented in ways directly relevant to Regen Atlas as an environmental intelligence platform for bioregional capital coordination.

The paper "Infrastructuring Pop-Up Cities with Social Layer" (arxiv:2511.15680, Ji & Hu, 2025) studied deployments across ShanHaiWoo, muChiangmai, Edge Esmeralda, Aleph, and Gathering of Tribe. A separate "Pop-up City Report" (referenced as 2605.30109v1 in user notes) surveys 107 intentional temporary cities with 18 recurring design decisions and governance patterns ranging from founder dictatorship to liquid democracy.

## TIER 1: High-Relevance -- Direct Environmental/Regenerative Mission

These organizations have explicit ecological mandates and would be strong onboarding candidates.

### 1. NetX State (NetX Gen)

- **Location:** Patagonia, Argentina (primary); expanding to Brazil, Caribbean
- **Website:** [netxgen.world](https://www.netxgen.world) / [netxstate.com](https://www.netxstate.com)
- **Contact:** Valentina Faynbloch (Regenerative Economist, Nodes Project Manager); active on [Regen Coordination Hub](https://hub.regencoordination.xyz/t/from-node-to-mycelium-web3-refi-onboarding-for-regen-nodes-latam/399)
- **Why relevant:**
  - Explicitly building "ReGen Nodes" -- physical territories with organized communities working toward ecological, economic, and cultural regeneration of their bioregion
  - Long-term goal: first "Ciudad Regenerativa" (Regenerative City) in Patagonia
  - Developing ZEEIR -- Special Economic Zones for Regenerative Impact -- with "regeneration as the main KPI"
  - Uses ReFi (Web3 Regenerative Finance) tools to measure, verify, and finance ecosystem and community impacts
  - Co-founded ReFi Argentina; launching first Web3 onboarding program in LATAM for non-Web3 regenerative organizations
  - Co-hosted Regen Track at Ipe City (Florianopolis) with AgroforestDAO and RegenHub at DevConnect Buenos Aires
  - Participating organizations represent 100+ years of combined regenerative experience with strong territorial impact
- **Onboarding angle:** Their ReGen Nodes model maps directly to bioregional coordination. They need exactly the kind of environmental monitoring and capital coordination that Regen Atlas provides.

### 2. Regen Network

- **Location:** Global (decentralized), with land steward projects in Ecuador, Brazil, and worldwide
- **Website:** [regen.network](https://www.regen.network) / [registry.regen.network](https://registry.regen.network)
- **Contact:** Community via regen.network/community
- **Why relevant:**
  - Purpose-built blockchain platform for ecological monitoring, verification, and credit issuance
  - Regen Registry issues carbon credits, biodiversity credits, soil organic carbon credits, biochar credits, and more
  - Credit protocols specifically for biodiversity protection and environmental stewardship
  - MRV (Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification) infrastructure for ecological state and change of state
  - Active Environmental Stewardship Initiative supporting holistic regenerative practices with data collection
  - Projects monitoring jaguar biodiversity in Ecuador and Brazil with pre-sale biodiversity credits
- **Onboarding angle:** Infrastructure partner. Regen Network's registry and MRV tools complement Regen Atlas. Their land steward projects are natural listings, and their credit protocols could integrate with Regen Atlas's capital coordination layer.

## TIER 2: Strong Relevance -- Sustainability/Governance Focus with Ecological Components

### 8. Edge City

- **Location:** California, USA (HQ); villages in South Africa, Bhutan, Argentina, Thailand
- **Website:** [edgecity.live](https://www.edgecity.live)
- **Contact:** Timour Kosters, Janine Leger (co-founders); via website
- **Why relevant:**
  - "Society incubator" running monthlong pop-up villages globally
  - Climate Tech in Africa Panel at Edge City South Africa (featuring Kinjani, EcoRestore, BoomBio, XAU)
  - Edge Esmeralda 2025: off-grid solar datacenter built by residents (solar-powered, battery-backed GPU compute)
  - Partnership with Bhutan's Gelephu Mindfulness City on sustainability and consciousness
  - Visited biotech labs doing sustainable manufacturing via precision fermentation
  - Ecosystem includes Charter Cities Institute (climate change, urbanization) and Stripe Climate (carbon removal)
  - Design principle "Default Healthy" includes environmental well-being
- **Onboarding angle:** Their village network is a testbed for environmental tech. Climate tech panels and off-grid infrastructure experiments make them a natural Regen Atlas partner. Their ecosystem projects (Stripe Climate, Charter Cities Institute) are additional listing candidates.

### 9. ZuKas (ZuKas)

- **Location:** Kas, Turkey
- **Website:** [zukascity.com](https://zukascity.com) / [zukas.city](https://www.zukas.city)
- **Contact:** Via website; 200-person residency

- **Onboarding angle:** Their DePIN + ReFi combination is directly relevant to environmental monitoring infrastructure. Community-owned sensor networks could feed into Regen Atlas data layers.

### 10. ZuCity Japan

  - Art residencies and community infrastructure in rural Japan
- **Onboarding angle:** Adaptive reuse / circular economy model relevant to bioregional revitalization. Rural restoration with sustainability metrics could be tracked on Regen Atlas.

### 11. Ipe City

### 12. Gelephu Mindfulness City (GMC)

- **Location:** Gelephu, Bhutan
- **Website:** [gmc.bt](https://gmc.bt)
- **Contact:** Government of Bhutan initiative; via official channels
- **Why relevant:**
  - State-backed city designed by Bjarke Ingels Group (BIG) integrating nature, sustainability, and mindfulness
  - Carbon-positive standard with locally sourced materials
  - Designed to "enhance ecological systems through an integrated network of wetlands, rivers, and forest corridors"
  - Plastic-free mandate
  - Bhutan already uses Gross National Happiness (GNH) instead of GDP -- ecological well-being is a core metric
  - Landscape architects leading planning with nature-based solutions
  - Edge City partnership: 30-person delegation explored consciousness and sustainability
- **Onboarding angle:** Government-scale regenerative city with environmental monitoring needs. Carbon-positive standard and ecological network design require exactly the kind of monitoring and verification Regen Atlas provides.
